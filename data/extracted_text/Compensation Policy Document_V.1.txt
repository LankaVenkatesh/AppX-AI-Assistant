COMPENSATION POLICY 
 
 
AppXcelerate Solutions Pvt Ltd 
Effective date: This policy is effective immediately from the date of issuance. 
 
 
1. Purpose 
The purpose of this Compensation Policy is to outline the structure, components, 
administration, and governance of employee compensation at AppXcelerate Solutions Pvt Ltd. 
It aims to ensure that all compensation practices are fair, transparent, consistent, and aligned 
with both industry standards and the organization’s objectives. In the event of any conflict 
between this policy and applicable statutory laws, the statutory provisions shall prevail. 
2. Scope 
This policy applies to all full -time, part -time, and contractual employees of AppXcelerate 
Solutions Pvt Ltd, unless stated otherwise in individual employment agreements. 
3. Compensation Structure 
 
Compensation at AppXcelerate Solutions Pvt Ltd is designed to be structured, equitable, and 
aligned with both organizational and individual performance. The total Cost to Company (CTC) 
may vary based on the employee’s role, grade, experience level, locatio n, and business unit 
requirements, ensuring flexibility to meet diverse operational and strategic needs while 
minimizing the risk of future disputes or ambiguity. 
The compensation structure may include one or more of the following components, as 
applicable: 
 
1. Fixed Compensation – This includes regular, recurring payments such as basic salary and 
fixed allowances paid on a monthly basis. 
2. Variable Pay / Performance -Based Pay  – Linked to individual, team, or company 
performance as per defined metrics and eligibility criteria. 
3. One-Time Payments – These may include joining bonuses, retention bonuses, special 
incentives, or other non-recurring payments approved by management. 
4. Statutory Components – Contributions and deductions mandated by applicable laws, 
such as Provident Fund, Professional Tax, ESI (if applicable), and other statutory 
benefits.

4. Fixed Compensation Components 
4.1 Basic Pay 
• At AppXcelerate Solutions Pvt Ltd, Basic Pay constitutes the primary and most 
significant fixed component of an employee’s salary. The determination and 
structure of Basic Pay are governed by applicable statutory provisions and 
labour regulations in India,  which may vary across states and are subject to 
periodic amendments by the respective authorities. Accordingly, the company 
aligns its salary structure with the relevant statutory framework applicable to 
the employee’s designated work location. 
• For employees based in Bangalore, Karnataka, Basic Pay is currently structured 
at 50% of the employee’s Gross Pay , in line with commonly accepted industry 
practices and internal compensation guidelines, ensuring compliance with 
minimum wage and PF laws. 
• For the purpose of this calculation, Gross Pay includes Basic, HRA, and fixed 
allowances, and excludes Employer PF and insurance components. 
• The adopted structure supports full compliance with applicable labour laws, 
including regulations related to minimum wages, provident fund contributions, 
gratuity, and other statutory benefits. It also ensures fairness, parity, and 
internal equity across roles, designations, grades, and departments within the 
organization, while remaining competitive within the broader job market. 
• Basic Pay is a fixed and guaranteed monthly amount, payable irrespective of the 
employee’s or the organization’s performance. It forms the fundamental basis 
for calculating statutory and long -term benefits such as Provident Fund 
contributions, Gratuity, Bonus (where applicable), Leave Encashment, and other 
terminal or recurring benefits as required under law and company policy. 
 
4.2 House Rent Allowance (HRA) 
House Rent Allowance (HRA) is a component of an employee’s compensation package intended 
to support rental accommodation expenses. HRA is calculated at 40% of the employee’s Basic 
Salary. Any revision in Basic Salary will automatically impact the HRA amount. 
HRA forms an important part of the Cost to Company (CTC) and take -home salary, as it is 
partially tax-exempt under Section 10(13A) of the Income Tax Act for employees residing in 
rented accommodation. To claim tax exemption on HRA, employees must submit va lid rent 
receipts, a rental agreement, and the landlord’s PAN card for annual rent exceeding ₹1,00,000, 
in accordance with statutory requirements. 
Eligibility Criteria: 
* The employee must be living in rented accommodation (not company-provided housing). 
* All required documentation must be submitted within stipulated timelines. 
* The employee must meet applicable statutory requirements.

4.3 Special Allowance 
• The Special Allowance is a fully taxable part of an employee’s salary that helps adjust 
the total compensation to meet company and market standards. 
• It is used to balance pay when other specific allowances do not apply and can include 
small or miscellaneous allowances that are not listed separately. This allowance gives 
the company flexibility when designing or revising salary structures. 
• It may be updated during salary reviews, restructuring, or when policies or statutory 
guidelines change. 
 
4.4 Niche Skills Allowance / Skill Premium 
• This component is designed to recognize and reward the specialized expertise that our 
employees bring to the organization and that significantly contributes to our projects, 
clients, and overall business success. 
Eligibility Criteria 
• Employees become eligible for the Niche Skills Allowance upon completion of two (2) 
years of continuous service with the organization. 
• Employees completing two years during an ongoing quarter will receive the allowance 
on a pro-rata basis, based on the eligible period within the same quarter. 
• Must have joined AppXcelerate Solutions Pvt. Ltd. as a Fresher 
 
Payment Guidelines 
• The Niche Skills Allowance is paid on a quarterly basis. The amount may vary across 
roles, technologies, and business requirements, based on organizational priorities and 
project demand. 
• Employees who have resigned or are serving their notice period during a given quarter 
are not eligible for the payout for that quarter. 
5. Variable Pay

defined variable pay percentage as stated in their offer or appointment letter. The variable pay 
is performance -linked, evaluated on the basis of individual performance, including 
achievement of KRAs, KPIs, and behavioral alignment. The assessment and payout shall take 
place on a half -yearly basis, covering April to September (paid in October) and  October to 
March (paid in April), and the final payout amount will be det ermined based on performance 
ratings, period served, and management approval. Employees must be on the active payroll at 
the time of payout, meet the minimum performance threshold, and must not be under a 
Performance Improvement Plan (PIP), disciplinary action, or notice period unless otherwise

6. One-Time Payments 
6.1 Joining Bonus 
At AppXcelerate, we are committed to attracting and welcoming exceptional talent who can 
contribute meaningfully to our organization’s growth and success. In certain cases, based on 
specific business, project, or role requirements, the company may offer a Joining Bonus as a 
one-time benefit to support an employee’s transition into the organization. 
This initiative reflects our intent to remain competitive in the market while recognizing and 
valuing specialized skills and experience. 
Note: The Joining Bonus is offered selectively and is not a standard component of all 
employment offers. Its applicability is determined by business needs, role criticality, market 
conditions, and the candidate’s profile. 
Purpose of the Joining Bonus 
The Joining Bonus may be offered to: 
• Compensate for notice period buyout or other transition-related expenses 
• Support relocation or role-change requirements 
• Attract niche-skilled or high-demand talent 
• Strengthen key teams and priority projects 
• This benefit is provided as a one -time payment in appreciation of the expertise and  
value an employee brings to the organization. 
• This shall be payable upon successful completion of 3 months in AppX. 
Eligibility & Commitment 
Employees who are offered a Joining Bonus are expected to remain with AppXcelerate for a 
minimum period of twelve (12) months from their date of joining. This represents a mutual 
commitment to stability, collaboration, and long-term contribution. 
Recovery Clause 
• In the event the Employee resigns from employment or the employment is terminated 
by the Company, for any reason whatsoever, prior to the completion of twelve (12) 
months of continuous service, the Employee shall be required to refund the entire 
amount of the Joining Bonus to the Company 
• The recoverable amount may be adjusted against the employee’s Final Settlement. Any 
remaining balance must be paid on or before the last working day. In the event of non- 
payment, the organization reserves the right to initiate appropriate recovery 
proceedings in accordance with applicable laws and internal policies.

contractual obligations, as determined by management and in accordance with applicable 
laws. 
The employee expressly authorizes the Company to recover and/or deduct  any such 
recoverable amounts from salary, incentives, reimbursements, benefits, or the Full and 
Final settlement payable to the employee, without requiring any further consent. In the 
event that such amounts cannot be fully recovered through the above mea ns, the 
employee shall remain liable to repay the outstanding balance to the Company within the 
timeline communicated by the HR or Finance department.

Other Salary Components 
Salary Advance 
The AppX Salary Advance Policy outlines the guidelines under which employees of 
AppXcelerate Solutions Pvt. Ltd. may request a salary advance to support genuine financial 
needs such as relocation, health (self and immediate family), children’s education, ma rriage 
(self and immediate family), or court -related expenses. The policy is applicable only to 
permanent employees who have completed a minimum of six months of continuous full-time 
service and who do not have any outstanding salary advance; employees on contract or serving 
notice period are not eligible. The advance amount shall not exceed 40% of the employee’s CTC 
or ₹2,00,000, whichever is lower, and may be availed only once in a twelve -month period. 
Employees must submit a written request to HR stating the reason for the advance, which will 
be subject to approval by HR, Finance, and senior management, at their discretion. The 
approved advance shall be recovered in a maximum of six, nine, or twelve equal monthly 
instalments, starting from the subsequent payroll cycle, with deductions made  directly from 
salary; for early repayment, the employee must notify HR and  the Finance department on or 
before the 15th of the respective month. No interest shall be charged on the salary advance, 
and all applicable tax regulations shall be complied with. In exceptional cases of extreme  
financial hardship, management may approve a higher amount or extended repayment terms 
on a case -by-case basis, with such exceptions duly documented and approved by the HR 
department, whose decision shall be final. All salary advance requests shall be handled with 
strict confidentiality, and this policy shall be periodically reviewed and may be amended at the 
company’s discretion.

7. Statutory Components Applicable in Karnataka 
 
7.1 Provident Fund (PF) 
Provident Fund (PF) is a retirement benefit scheme for employees, where both the employer 
and the employee contribute a fixed portion of the employee’s salary to a fund that grows over 
time with interest. It is a mandatory benefit in many countries, including India, under the 
Employees’ Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. 
Key Points: 
• Purpose: PF is meant to provide financial security to employees after retirement, during 
unemployment, or in case of emergencies. 
• Eligibility: Typically, applicable to salaried employees earning a basic salary and 
allowances as defined under PF rules. 
• Contribution: Both employer and employee contribute a fixed amount or percentage of 
the employee’s basic salary to the PF account. 
• Interest: The PF balance earns interest, which is compounded annually. 
• Withdrawal: Employees may withdraw their Provident Fund (PF) amount upon 
retirement or resignation, or for specific purposes such as medical emergencies, 
housing, or education, in accordance with the prescribed Provident Fund withdrawal 
rules 
• At AppX both employer and employee contributes Rs 1800 each month towards PF.
